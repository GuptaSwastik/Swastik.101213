Types of space exploration mission

Manned Missions: Human Spaceflight: Involving astronauts in space for scientific research, technology testing, or space tourism. Space Station Missions: Establishing and maintaining space stations like the International Space Station (ISS) for long-duration experiments. Lunar Missions: Exploring the Moon, either with manned landers and rovers or with robotic probes. Unmanned Missions: Flyby Missions: Spacecraft passes by a celestial body without entering orbit for initial reconnaissance. Orbiter Missions: Spacecraft enters and orbits a celestial body for detailed, long-term observation. Lander Missions: Spacecraft descends to the surface of a celestial body for landing and exploration. Rover Missions: Self-propelled vehicles that travel across the surface of a celestial body, collecting data and samples. Probe Missions: Include atmospheric probes, penetrator probes (impact probes), and sample return missions. Observatory Missions: Space telescopes and observatories for astronomical observations. Earth Observation Missions: Satellites and probes for studying Earth's atmosphere, climate, and surface. Communication and Navigation Missions: Satellites for telecommunications and navigation. Examples of Space Missions: Apollo Program: A series of manned missions, culminating in the first lunar landing with Apollo 11. International Space Station (ISS): A cooperative research facility in Earth orbit. Voyager Missions: Flyby missions to outer planets, including Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Mars Missions: Exploration of Mars through orbiters, landers, and rovers like the Mars 2020 mission with the Perseverance rover and Ingenuity helicopter. Europa Clipper: A mission to explore Jupiter's moon Europa, investigating its potential ocean. BepiColombo: A mission to explore Mercury.
